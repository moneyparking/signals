Executive Determination — Growth Signal Validity

Independent Growth Signal Assessment · Executive summary · Illustrative reference

Context

This determination addresses whether the signal currently used to justify scaling paid acquisition remains economically predictive at the present level of spend and revenue.

Such determinations are typically required when acquisition metrics remain stable while revenue outcomes no longer scale proportionally, creating divergence in internal interpretations and increased capital allocation risk.

Signal Reviewed

Scaling decision signal: Cost Per Lead (CPL)

In this illustrative scenario, CPL stability continued to be used as a primary trigger for scaling decisions despite emerging lag effects and a weakening relationship with realized revenue.

Assessment Basis

The determination considers whether the signal continues to function as a reliable proxy for downstream revenue outcomes at scale, with specific attention to:

  • Signal–revenue consistency across time periods
  • Material lag between acquisition activity and revenue realization
  • Revenue stability relative to incremental spend

Determination

SIGNAL NO LONGER ECONOMICALLY PREDICTIVE

Based on the assessed conditions, the signal used to justify scaling does not remain economically predictive at the current scale.

Continued reliance on this signal materially increases the risk of inefficient capital allocation and distorted executive decision-making.

Scope & Limitations

  • One-time, fixed-scope independent determination
  • No optimization, remediation, or alternative signals provided
  • No implementation or advisory services included
This executive determination is provided to inform senior decision-making. It does not constitute consulting, advisory, financial, or operational services and does not replace internal judgment or accountability.
