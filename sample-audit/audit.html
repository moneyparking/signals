1. Scope of Assessment

This document illustrates the format, tone, and substance of an Independent Growth Signal Assessment determining whether a growth signal used for scaling decisions remains economically predictive of revenue outcomes.

This type of determination is typically required when scaling decisions continue to rely on stable acquisition metrics while revenue outcomes no longer respond proportionally. At this stage, internal interpretations often diverge, and an independent determination is required to support capital allocation and executive decision-making.

This is an illustrative sample only. A live assessment is conducted as a fixed-scope, one-time engagement based exclusively on aggregated performance signals provided during intake.

2. Signal Assessed

Scaling decision signal under assessment:

Cost Per Lead (CPL)

In this illustrative scenario, scaling decisions continued to rely on CPL stability despite the presence of material lag effects and a weakening relationship between lead acquisition costs and realized revenue.

3. Basis for Determination

The assessment evaluates whether the signal continues to function as a reliable proxy for downstream revenue outcomes at the current level of scale.

In particular, the determination considers:

  • Consistency of the signal–revenue relationship across time windows
  • Presence and persistence of material lag effects
  • Stability of revenue realization relative to scaling actions
Assessment condition:

If the scaling signal remains stable
while revenue realization materially lags beyond
acceptable system thresholds,
the signal is no longer economically predictive.

4. Determination

Determination: SIGNAL NO LONGER PREDICTIVE

Based on the assessed conditions, the signal used to justify scaling does not remain economically predictive at the current level of spend and revenue.

Continued reliance on this signal materially increases the risk of inefficient capital allocation and distorted scaling decisions.

5. Limitations & Exclusions

  • This assessment does not include optimization or remediation guidance
  • No alternative signals are proposed
  • No implementation, operational, or advisory services are provided
  • This document is an illustrative example only and not a live assessment
