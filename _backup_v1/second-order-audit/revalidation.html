Economic Signal Revalidation

This memo builds on a prior independent verdict indicating insufficient signal validity for scaling decisions.

1. Context Recap

A prior economic signal audit concluded that the primary scaling signal did not maintain sufficient causal correlation with realized revenue.

This memo does not reassess that verdict. It evaluates the conditions under which revalidation may become possible.

2. Constraint Identification

  • Attribution lag exceeding reliable decision windows
  • High variance in activation-to-revenue conversion
  • Sales cycle inconsistency across cohorts

These constraints limit the applicability of the current scaling signal as a deterministic decision input.

3. Boundary Conditions

Scaling may be considered conditionally safe if and only if the following conditions are met.
Signal lag ≤ X days Conversion variance ≤ Y% Revenue attribution confidence ≥ Z

These thresholds define a decision-safe envelope, not performance targets.

4. Scenario Envelope

Scenario A — Conservative

Scaling permissible only within narrow spend bounds.

Scenario B — Neutral

Scaling conditionally permissible with monitored exposure.

Scenario C — Aggressive

Scaling not recommended due to elevated residual risk.

5. Residual Risk Statement

Even if boundary conditions are met, residual uncertainty remains due to factors outside the scope of this assessment.

This memo defines decision boundaries, not outcome guarantees.

This document is intended for internal decision-making only. It does not replace independent judgement or fiduciary responsibility.
