2. Signal Under Review

Primary scaling trigger evaluated:

  • Cost Per Lead (CPL)

The organization continued scaling paid acquisition based on CPL stability, despite delayed and weakening revenue response downstream.

3. Rule Evaluation (excerpt)

Rule ID: S-04 IF scaling trigger = CPL AND revenue lag > 2 sales cycles THEN signal validity = broken

This rule tests whether a proxy metric used for scaling decisions remains causally correlated with realized revenue outcomes.

4. Economic Impact Calculation

Loss source

Loss is attributed to continued spend beyond the point where CPL ceased to reflect marginal revenue efficiency.

Baseline marginal CAC: $980 Scaled marginal CAC: $1,420 Marginal inefficiency: $440 per deal Annualized deal volume: 310 Estimated annual loss: $110,000 – $160,000

Loss range reflects sensitivity to attribution lag and deal conversion variance across observed periods.

5. Economic Verdict

Verdict: SIGNAL INVALID

Scaling decisions based on CPL are no longer economically justified under current system conditions.

Continued scaling without signal correction is expected to generate incremental economic loss.
